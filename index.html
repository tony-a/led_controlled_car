Led controlled car

Idea

An attempt at controlling an RC car by drawing out a path using an LED on top of several photoresistors.

 

Parts & Libraries

Car hardware:
  • Arduino Uno
  • Arduino Motor Shield S3
  • nRF24L01+ (as receiver)
Controller hardware:
  • Arduino Leonardo
  • Photoresistor (x2)
  • nRF24L01+ (as transmitter)
  • Fancy LED-wand I made.
    • I recommend using a supersonic screwdriver instead.
Libraries:
  • For the RF24 modules I used this library by @tmrh20.
  • For the Motor shield, I wrote my own (very basic) library, link.

Problems Encountered

  1. Setting up the RF24 on the Leonardo board requires different wiring than the Uno.
    Spent a few days scratching my head before relaizing that. Basically you have to use the 6 separate ICSP pins on the board, rather than pins 11, 12 and 13 as most docs say.
    This site has good a diagram showing the proper connections to the ICSP pins.

  2. The Motor Shield uses pins 11, 12 and 13 on the arduino, which are the ICSP pins used by RF24 module (as mentioned above).
    The problem is discussed in this stackexchange. I ended up bending the three pins up (on the motor shield), and rewired pin-12 to Arduino pin-2, MODE=OUTPUT, as seen below.
    I also used 90-degrees exentsion pins to access the ICSP pins and hook up the RF24 piece (picture on the right).

Idea Motivation

An attempt at making a car follow me (more specifically to follow a hand above it.)
Turned into an attempt at making a car replicate my hand gesture.
Turned into a car replicating the path of an LED over several photoresistors.

Conclusion

Overall it was a fun learning experience. Wrote my first library.
The car doesn't do much yet, maybe I'll add flying functionality next.
