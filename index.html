Led controlled car

Download .zip Download .tar.gz View on GitHub

Idea

An attempt at controlling an RC car by drawing out a path using an LED on top of several photoresistors.

Parts & Libraries

Car hardware:
  • Arduino Uno
  • Arduino Motor Shield S3
  • RF2fl01+ (as receiver)
Controller hardware:
  • Arduino Leonardo
  • Photoresistor (x2)
  • RF2fl01+ (as transmitter)
Libraries:
  • For the RF24 modules I used this library by @tmrh20.
  • For the Motor shield, I wrote my own (very basic) library; Link.

Problems Encountered

  1. Setting up RF24 with Leonardo requires different wiring than the Uno.
    Spent a few days scratching my head before relaizing that.
  2. The Motor Shield S3 uses pins 11, 12 and 13 on the arduino, which are also the ICSP pins. but the ICSP pins are required by the RF24 shield. I ended up bending the three pins up, and rewired pin-12 to Arduino pin-2 MODE=OUTPUT.

Current Work

I'm currently adding turn functionality to the car by adding a third photoresistor.

Future Modifications

  • Use light intensity to control speed of car.
  • Add turn functionality.
  • Upgrade LED-pencil controller.

Idea Motivation

An attempt at making a car follow me (more specifically to follow a hand above it.)
Turned into an attempt at making a car replicate my hand gesture.
Turned into a car replicating the path of an LED over several photoresistors.
